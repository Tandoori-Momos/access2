How does it work?
Hyperloop is a new mode of transportation that moves
freight and people quickly, safely, on-demand and direct
from origin to destination. Passengers or cargo are load
into the Hyperloop vehicle and accelerate gradually via
electric propulsion through a low-pressure tube.
The vehicle floats above the track using magnetic levitation
and glides at airline speeds for long distances due to ultra
low aerodynamic drag. Hyperloop One systems will be built
on columns or tunneled below ground to avoid dangerous
grade crossings and wildlife.
